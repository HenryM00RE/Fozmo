# Code of conduct

Fozmo should be a constructive place to collaborate. Participants are expected
to be respectful, patient and specific, especially when discussing subjective
audio behavior, unfamiliar hardware or work from a first-time contributor.

## Expected behavior

- Give technical feedback about the work, not the person.
- Assume good faith while still asking for reproducible evidence.
- Welcome different experience levels and make room for questions.
- Respect privacy and do not repost personal, account, network or device data.
- Accept maintainer decisions about project scope, release risk and licence
  boundaries without harassment.

Harassment, threats, discriminatory language, sexualized attention, deliberate
disclosure of private information, sustained disruption, impersonation and
retaliation are not acceptable.

This policy applies to repository issues, pull requests, discussions, release
coordination and other spaces where someone is representing the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

For an urgent safety concern, use the private reporting channel described in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) and prefix the summary with **Code of conduct**. If
the private form is unavailable, open a minimal issue requesting private
maintainer contact without naming people or describing the incident publicly.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue a warning, limit participation,
temporarily suspend access or permanently ban a participant. Reports will be
handled as privately as reasonably possible, and retaliation against a
reporter is itself a violation. A maintainer who is the subject of a report
must not make the enforcement decision alone when another maintainer is
available.
